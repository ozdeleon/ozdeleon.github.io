Loteria game

2019 • Card game to learn Spanish • California

Personal Project

A take on the game of Lotería used as a tool to teach children Spanish.


What the heck is Lotería?

Glad you asked! Lotería is a traditional game similar to Bingo. It was created in Mexico, but over the years it's made its way to several Latin American countries, as well as Europe.

Each player gets a board with a randomly created 4 × 4 grid of unique images. One person is in charge of calling out the name of an image from one of 54 cards. The first person to get all 16 images on their board and shout out "Lotería!" wins.

I was introduced to the game by my grandmother. Every time she'd visit, we'd bust it out. Those memories are some of the best, and now I'm making new memories by playing the game with my own kids. Needless to say, this project is near and dear to my heart.

Goal


My goal was to use bold, simple shapes combined with intriguing color palettes, woven into simple imagery to facilitate learning.

Design

Approach


I read an article in the Journal of Experimental Child Psychology about pictures and words being used as stimulus and response items in paired-associate learning for young children. Abstract shapes help commit these words into memory. Kids have to figure out what the shapes and colors create and associate that with specific words and images, challenging their cognitive skills.
